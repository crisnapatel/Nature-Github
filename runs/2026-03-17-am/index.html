Sustainable Energy & Fuels Q1 04 Mar 2026

DFT-D3 and ab initio molecular dynamics investigation of hydrogen storage in Li-decorated carbon-doped BN analogue of graphyne

Couples dispersion-corrected DFT adsorption analysis with AIMD stability checks on a Li-decorated BN-graphyne analogue. The workflow is especially relevant for 2D hydrogen-storage claims that need to survive finite-temperature validation.

Why it matters: a compact example of how adsorption screening and thermal stability testing should reinforce each other.

Journal of Chemical Theory and Computation Q1 24 Feb 2026

Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials Enable Molecular Dynamics Simulations of Doped MoS2

Builds a doped-MoS2-specific MLIP workflow so simulations can escape routine DFT length and time limits without retreating to an overly simplified pristine-sheet model.

Why it matters: a strong reference for larger-scale MD on compositionally perturbed 2D materials.

Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling Q1 24 Jan 2026

A Transferable Force Field for Simulating Adsorption in MOFs with Open Metal Sites Based on 12-6-4 Lennard-Jones Potential

Targets the open-metal-site failure mode in adsorption simulations, replacing generic force-field behavior with a transferable 12-6-4 treatment better suited to exposed metal centers.

Why it matters: directly actionable for porous-material screening where coordinatively unsaturated sites dominate uptake behavior.

Open paper
Journal of Materials Chemistry A Q1 02 Jan 2026

Accelerating discovery through integration: a DFT validated machine learning framework for screening MOF photocatalysts

Integrates machine-learning ranking with DFT validation for MOF photocatalyst discovery, keeping first-principles checks in the loop while still broadening the searchable candidate space.

Why it matters: a useful blueprint for porous-material discovery workflows that need both throughput and physically grounded screening.
