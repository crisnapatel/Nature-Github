17 March 2026 selection

npj Computational Materials Q1 06 Mar 2026

Computational study of density fluctuation-facilitated shear band formation in bulk metallic glasses

Atomistic analysis ties density heterogeneity to shear-band nucleation and propagation in metallic glasses. The paper is immediately useful for MD studies that track local free volume, non-affine motion, and history-dependent plastic localization.

Why it matters: a strong reference for linking structural fluctuations to deformation pathways in disordered materials.

Open paper
Sustainable Energy & Fuels Q1 04 Mar 2026

DFT-D3 and ab initio molecular dynamics investigation of hydrogen storage in Li-decorated carbon-doped BN analogue of graphyne

Combines dispersion-corrected DFT and AIMD to evaluate adsorption energetics and thermal stability in a Li-functionalized 2D storage platform. It is a compact example of how to couple favorable adsorption energies to finite-temperature validation.

Why it matters: useful for 2D adsorption workflows that need both static site analysis and AIMD stability checks.

Open paper
Journal of Chemical Theory and Computation Q1 24 Feb 2026

Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials Enable Molecular Dynamics Simulations of Doped MoS2

Extends MLIP-driven MD from pristine 2D materials to doped MoS2, which is much closer to device and catalysis use cases than idealized monolayers. The setup is directly relevant to LAMMPS or DeePMD users interested in dopant-driven disorder and transport.

Why it matters: a practical path for larger-scale atomistics in chemically perturbed layered systems.

Open paper
npj Computational Materials Q1 19 Feb 2026

Accurate screening of functional materials with machine-learning potential and transfer-learned regressions: Heusler alloy benchmark

Benchmarks a pipeline that combines machine-learning potentials with transfer-learned regressions for scalable functional-material screening in a broad Heusler-alloy space.

Why it matters: a clean example of how to hand off from atomistic sampling to property ranking without reverting to all-DFT throughput.

Open paper
npj Computational Materials Q1 16 Feb 2026

Incongruent melting and phase diagram of SiC from machine learning molecular dynamics

Uses ML molecular dynamics to extract melting and phase-behavior information for SiC at temperatures and system sizes that are hard to reach with direct first-principles MD.

Why it matters: a strong model for phase-diagram and coexistence studies in refractory or extreme-condition materials.

Open paper
npj Computational Materials Q1 06 Feb 2026

Efficient and accurate spatial mixing of machine learned interatomic potentials for materials science

Introduces spatially mixed MLIPs for systems where different local environments benefit from different learned models, rather than a single global potential.

Why it matters: particularly relevant for interfaces, defects, and multiregion simulations that strain monolithic potential fits.

Open paper
npj Computational Materials Q1 05 Feb 2026

Atomistic understanding of hydrogen bubble-induced embrittlement in tungsten enabled by machine learning molecular dynamics

Brings machine-learning molecular dynamics to hydrogen-bubble damage in tungsten, linking defect evolution and stress concentration to embrittlement in a fusion-material context.

Why it matters: a large-scale defect-dynamics reference for gas, radiation, and implantation problems in refractory metals.

Open paper
npj Computational Materials Q1 26 Jan 2026

Self-optimizing machine learning potential assisted automated workflow for complex materials design

Pushes MLIP-assisted materials discovery toward an automated, self-improving loop rather than a hand-tuned sequence of training, validation, and screening steps.

Why it matters: highly relevant to active-learning and DP-GEN-like workflows for complex chemistries.

Open paper
Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling Q1 24 Jan 2026

A Transferable Force Field for Simulating Adsorption in MOFs with Open Metal Sites Based on 12-6-4 Lennard-Jones Potential

Parameterizes a transferable force field for adsorption in MOFs with open metal sites using a 12-6-4 form to improve the treatment of exposed metal centers.

Why it matters: directly actionable for adsorption screening and GCMC workflows where generic force fields miss open-metal-site physics.

Open paper
Journal of Materials Chemistry A Q1 02 Jan 2026

Accelerating discovery through integration: a DFT validated machine learning framework for screening MOF photocatalysts

Couples DFT validation to machine-learning ranking for porous photocatalyst discovery in MOFs, keeping electronic-structure checks in the loop while expanding the searchable candidate space.

Why it matters: a useful pattern for framework screening where structure-property mapping still needs physically grounded descriptors.
